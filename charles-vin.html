Your Questions, Answered

Everything you need to know before booking your VIN inspection.

Do I need a VIN verification to register my out-of-state car in Florida?

+
Yes. Florida requires a physical VIN verification for all vehicles titled in another state before they can be registered here. This must be performed by a Florida DHSMV-licensed inspector (not a police officer or DMV clerk).

How long does the inspection take?

+
The on-site inspection typically takes 15â€“30 minutes. We verify the VIN on the dashboard, door jamb, and engine bay, cross-reference with official databases, and complete all required documentation on the spot. Your certified paperwork is ready within 24â€“48 hours (same-day rush available).

What documents do I need to have ready?

+
You'll need: the original out-of-state title, your government-issued photo ID, and the vehicle itself. For rebuilt/salvage titles, you'll also need repair receipts and photos. We'll send you a complete checklist when you book.

Do you inspect boats, motorcycles, and trailers?

+
Absolutely. We inspect all types of vehicles including motorcycles, scooters, boats, jet skis, travel trailers, utility trailers, RVs, and commercial vehicles. Each vehicle type has specific Florida requirements, and we're experts in all of them.

This has never happened. Our 100% DMV acceptance rate speaks for itself. But if it ever did, we'd redo your inspection at absolutely no charge. We stand behind our work completely.
