  • Anxiety
  • Autism
  • Trauma
  • Sensory issues
  • Physical difficulties
  • Motor problems and delays

This advanced adaptive aquatic training gives our instructors the tools to teach every swimmer with confidence and care.

Special Lessons for Special Students

Raising a child with special needs comes with unique challenges, but learning to swim doesn't have to be one of them. At LowCountry Swim School, we believe swimming is a life skill® that makes children safer and happier.

We offer private swim lessons for children with unique needs, including autism, sensory challenges, ADHD, physical disabilities, and more. Swimming not only improves safety, but it can also be therapeutic—helping with sensory overload, providing a sense of weightlessness, and relieving physical discomfort.

Lessons also support balance, coordination, strength, and range of motion, while often boosting confidence and self-esteem.

Will My Child with Special Needs Benefit from Swim Lessons?

All children should learn water safety and how to swim when possible. Drowning is the #1 cause of accidental death in children with autism, and many kids are naturally drawn to pools, lakes, and even bathtubs.

Children with special needs process the world differently and benefit from flexible, child-focused teaching. Our LowCountry Swim School Certified™ Instructors have experience with a variety of needs, including:

  • Autism, Asperger's, and related developmental syndromes
  • Cerebral palsy
  • Down syndrome
  • Sensory processing challenges
  • Physical difficulties and disabilities

Before your child's first lesson, we'll discuss your family's goals, learn about your child's abilities, and answer any questions. You can even meet the team beforehand. Then we'll create a customized teaching plan and match your child with an instructor who's the right personality fit.
