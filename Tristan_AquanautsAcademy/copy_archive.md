### ABOUT US

**Our Story:**

"Aquanauts Swim Academy was born out of both challenge and inspiration."

One of my clearest memories of swimming is clinging to my dad's shoulders as he swam us across one of the lakes here underwater. The feeling of weightlessness underwater creating joy and excitement as his strokes pulled us forward—it felt like a "space shuttle ride" under the surface of that water. In that moment, I felt safe, free, and inspired by the feeling of being underwater. That memory has stayed with me, shaping how I see swimming: not just as a skill, but as an adventure that builds joy and confidence.

However, in October of 2023, my life changed suddenly when my dad was rushed to the hospital and passed away from stage 4 cancer. At just 23 years old, I had to step away from university to take care of my younger siblings and rethink my path forward. It was an incredibly difficult time, but it also became the moment where I realized I could turn my lifelong love of swimming into something meaningful—not just for me, but for families across Vancouver Island.

At 23, with my dad's memory as my guide, I started teaching private lessons. What began as doing some private lessons on the side has grown into a thriving community program and business, reaching hundreds of families and swimmers of all abilities across Vancouver Island. My goal has always been to create more than just swim lessons—I want every child and family to discover the same confidence, joy, and freedom in the water that I found growing up.

Aquanauts Swim Academy is more than just a business to me—it's a legacy. It's a way to honor my dad, who first put me in the water, and to inspire the next generation of swimmers to build resilience, joy, and lifelong skills with every splash, with every jump and with every stroke. Every time I see a child smile in the water, I'm reminded of that space shuttle ride with my dad, and I know we're carrying his spirit forward!

### Frequently Asked Questions

#### General

**Q: What types of swim lessons does Aquanauts offer?**

A: Aquanauts offers a variety of swim lessons designed to accommodate different learning preferences and abilities. These include Private lessons (1:1), which provide personalized, one-on-one instruction focused entirely on the swimmer's individual goals and skill level. Semi-private lessons (1:2 or 1:3) are small group sessions ideal for siblings or friends who want to learn together while still receiving focused attention. Additionally, Aquanauts provides Adaptive swim lessons specifically tailored for swimmers with neurodiversabilities such as autism, sensory sensitivities, and mobility challenges, ensuring a supportive and inclusive environment that addresses unique needs. All lessons emphasize water safety and skill-building, reinforcing Aquanauts' commitment to effective, accessible swim education on Vancouver Island.

**Q: What ages do you teach?**

A: Aquanauts Swim Academy offers swim lessons for a wide range of ages, starting from infants as young as 6 months up to adults. Our programs are specifically designed to cater to each age group's developmental needs and swimming abilities. For young children and toddlers, we focus on water safety fundamentals and comfort in the water through engaging, gentle instruction. As children grow, lessons emphasize skill-building, stroke development, and endurance to build confidence and proficiency. Adult beginners receive personalized coaching that addresses their goals and comfort levels. Our flexible lesson formats—including private, semi-private, and mobile sessions—ensure that every student receives targeted instruction that aligns with their age, skill level, and individual needs.

**Q: How often should my child take swim lessons at Aquanauts?**

A: At Aquanauts Swim Academy, we recommend that children attend swim lessons at least once a week to build consistency and reinforce water safety skills effectively. Weekly lessons allow our instructors to assess progress regularly and tailor each session to the child's current abilities, ensuring steady improvement. For families seeking accelerated skill development, especially for younger or beginner swimmers, we offer the option of private lessons multiple times per week depending on availability. Consistent practice combined with our focused teaching approach promotes confidence and competence in the water, which is essential for your child's safety and enjoyment.

**Q: How long are the swim lessons at Aquanauts, and what can I expect during that time?**

A: At Aquanauts Swim Academy, each swim lesson is designed to be either 30 or 60 minutes long. The 30-minute sessions are most common for young children under 5, ensuring focused, individualized instruction that maximizes skill-building and water safety practice without overwhelming them. For older students, advanced swimmers, or those wanting a longer session, we offer 60-minute lessons. Whether taking private, semi-private, or mobile lessons, all our sessions provide enough time for warm-up, technique development, and confidence-building exercises tailored to your or your child's skill level. Our approach balances effective teaching with attention to each swimmer's unique needs, ensuring every lesson is productive and engaging.

**Q: What should I bring to my swimming lessons?**

A: For every lesson at Aquanauts Swim Academy, please bring a swimsuit and a towel as essentials. We also recommend swim goggles to protect your eyes and enhance visibility underwater, although they are optional. For children under three years old, a swim diaper is required to maintain pool hygiene. Additionally, bring a water bottle to stay hydrated throughout the session. Finally, come prepared with a positive attitude to make the most of your learning experience focused on water safety and skill-building.

**Q: What should I do if my child is afraid of the water?**

A: At Aquanauts Swim Academy, we understand that fear of water is common among children. Our instructors use gentle, confidence-building techniques tailored to each child's comfort level, ensuring they feel safe and supported throughout the learning process. We focus on creating a fun and encouraging environment, allowing children to progress gradually at their own pace. Our private, semi-private, and mobile lessons emphasize both water safety and skill development, designed to build your child's confidence and help them overcome water anxiety effectively.

**Q: Can siblings or friends take lessons together?**

A: Yes! We offer semi-private lessons (1:2 or 1:3) where siblings or friends of similar skill levels can learn together and enjoy the benefit of group lessons! Contact us to book semi-private lessons between two families.

**Q: What happens if my child misses a swimming lesson at Aquanauts?**

A: At Aquanauts, we understand that scheduling conflicts and unexpected events can occur. If your child misses a lesson, we offer one complimentary make-up lesson per session each month to ensure continued progress in their swim training. To accommodate make-up scheduling effectively, we kindly request at least 24 hours' notice for any cancellations. If notice is provided less than 2 hours before the lesson, a small cancellation fee may be applied to offset losses experienced by our instructors. This policy helps us maintain the quality and consistency of our private, semi-private, and mobile swim lessons focused on water safety and skill-building on Vancouver Island.

## /post/why-private-swim-lessons-are-worth-it (blog post)

# Why Private Swim Lessons Are Worth It

**Author:** Tristan Tolley
**Date:** Jul 16, 2025
**Read Time:** 4 min

If you are considering ways to enhance your swimming skills or help someone else improve, you may want to think about the benefits of personalized instruction. Individualized swim coaching can significantly improve proficiency in the water while also providing a myriad of other benefits. In this blog post, we will explore why private swim lessons can be a worthwhile investment for both children and adults.

### Individualized Swim Coaching: A Tailored Approach

One of the main reasons individualized swim coaching stands out is its tailored approach to learning. Unlike group lessons, where one instructor must cater to a larger audience, private lessons allow for focused instruction. Each student has unique strengths, weaknesses, and learning styles.

For instance, an individual may struggle with breathing techniques or swim stroke efficiency. In a private setting, the instructor can spend more time investigating these issues without distractions and provide specific drills and corrections tailored to the individual. This focused attention often leads to accelerated learning and improved performance.

Studies have shown that personalized instruction can lead to noticeable improvements in skill level within just a few lessons. According to a survey done by the National Swimming Coaches Association, participants in private lessons often see a 30-50% increase in their swimming capabilities over a series of sessions.

### Enhanced Safety and Comfort Levels

Swimming can be an intimidating experience for many, particularly for beginners or those with previous negative experiences. When students take private lessons, they often feel more comfortable and less distracted by the presence of peers.

This relaxation can significantly affect their learning process. Instructors can develop a rapport with students, which helps build trust. For example, if a child feels scared of putting their face in the water, the instructor can gradually guide them through practical exercises to overcome that fear.

Moreover, safety is paramount when teaching swimming. In a private lesson, instructors can pay immediate attention to potential dangers and adjust activities accordingly. Having a one-on-one experience means that the student becomes more aware of their surroundings and their capabilities in the water, ultimately fostering a safer swimming experience.

### What Age is Best for Private Swim Lessons?

When it comes to swim lessons, age can play a significant role in how effective the learning experience is. Many parents might wonder if their child is too young or too old to start taking swim lessons.

Generally, it is recommended that children can start taking private swim lessons as early as 2-3 years old. At this age, children are often comfortable in the water and can start learning basic skills such as floating and simple strokes. A private instruction setting is particularly beneficial for younger kids, as it allows them to learn at their own pace.

On the other hand, older children or adults can benefit significantly as well. Many adults, for example, may feel self-conscious in group settings and can find comfort in personalized coaching. Private lessons can help late learners gain confidence and skills at any age.

### Flexible Scheduling and Convenience

Another major advantage of individualized swim coaching is flexibility. With busy schedules often making it difficult to fit in regular classes, private lessons offer more convenient timing. Families can choose times that work for them, whether it's early morning, during the day, or even evening lessons.

This flexibility extends beyond scheduling as well. Instructors can also better adapt the course content based on an individual's needs at that moment. If a particular skill requires more attention, the lesson can focus on that area.

Ultimately, this adaptability can lead to more effective and efficient learning. It ensures that the student makes the most of their time in the water, getting a rich learning experience tailored specifically for them.

### Techniques, Skills, and Progress Tracking

Another significant benefit of individualized swim coaching is the opportunity for comprehensive skills assessment and enhancement. Instructors often use a mixture of techniques to track their students' progress, giving both the instructor and the student a better understanding of their growth.

For example, instructors might use video analysis for stroke technique, which allows students to visually comprehend where they need improvement. Additionally, they can set up specific goals for the student and regularly check in on their progress.

Detailed tracking encourages accountability and motivation. It shows students, whether they are children or adults, that improvement is possible with focused effort. When tangible progress is made, such as mastering a new stroke or completing a set distance, it often boosts the student's confidence, encouraging them to push further.

### Building Better Techniques

Another key aspect of individualized swim coaching is that it emphasizes quality over quantity. Rushing through skills in a group setting can lead to poor techniques being developed. Private lessons focus on the importance of form and technique from the beginning.

For instance, proper breathing patterns, body positioning, and arm strokes can be emphasized without the worry of a class agenda. This focus ultimately leads to better performance and effective water safety practices.

In addition, instructors can provide on-the-spot corrections and tips that promote better skills. Over time, these nuances can make a significant difference in the swimmer's overall ability.

### Investment in Future Enjoyment

Finally, think about swimming as not just a skill but a lifelong activity. The benefits of individualized swim coaching go beyond immediate skill enhancement. By investing in private swim lessons, you are encouraging a recreational activity that can be enjoyed throughout one's life.

Swimming is not only an excellent form of exercise; it is also often described as therapeutic. Many people find that being in the water can relieve stress and offer a sense of peace. Lessons taken at a young age can create a lasting love for swimming and water sports that can continue into adulthood.

Furthermore, proficient swimming skills can open doors to other water-based activities, such as diving, competitive swimming, and even lifeguarding. So, investing in private swim lessons today can yield numerous benefits and opportunities in the future.

### Are Private Swim Lessons Right for You?

Ultimately, individualized swim coaching presents a multitude of benefits that can cater to any individual's needs. Whether for a child afraid of the water, a beginner wanting to learn, or even an experienced swimmer eager to refine their skills, private lessons can be a game-changer.

If you're curious about how private swim lessons can benefit you or your loved ones, you can explore private swim lessons offered in your area. Remember, the journey of mastering swimming is not just about strokes and strokes. It's about building confidence, ensuring safety, and embarking on a lifetime of enjoyment in the water.

# Adult Swimming Classes: Learn New Skills

**By:** Tristan Tolley
**Date:** Jul 15, 2025
**Read time:** 4 min read

Swimming is not just a summer pastime; it is a vital life skill that offers numerous benefits, especially for adults. As people grow older, they often overlook the importance of learning how to swim, assuming it is too difficult or unnecessary. However, adult swimming classes provide an excellent opportunity to learn a valuable skill while also enjoying numerous physical and mental advantages. Whether you are a beginner or looking to refine your technique, there is much to gain from enrolling in a swimming class designed specifically for adults.

### The Value of Swimming Lessons for Grown-Ups

Swimming lessons for grown-ups are tailored to meet the unique needs and pace of adult learners. Unlike children's classes, these lessons focus on building confidence in the water, mastering the basics, and improving overall technique.

One of the primary benefits of taking adult swimming classes is the enjoyment of staying active. Regular swimming can improve cardiovascular health, build muscle tone, and enhance flexibility. For adults, particularly those who may have a more sedentary lifestyle, swimming offers a low-impact exercise option that minimizes the risk of injury. A study published by the American College of Sports Medicine found that "swimming regularly can lead to significant improvements in health markers, such as blood pressure and cholesterol levels."

### Building Confidence in the Water

Many adults feel hesitant about entering the water, especially if they did not learn to swim as children. Swimming classes create a supportive environment where individuals can overcome their fears and build confidence. Instructors are trained to recognize the unique challenges adult learners face and can provide personalized feedback to improve technique and comfort levels.

To boost self-assurance, swimming classes often include exercises focusing on safety and survival skills vital for any swimmer. This includes floating on the back, treading water, and understanding water safety protocols. The more comfortable you become in the water, the more likely you will enjoy swimming as a recreational activity.

### Is it Difficult to Learn Swimming After 40?

Learning to swim after 40 is feasible and can be a rewarding experience. While physical changes occur with age, your enthusiasm and motivation to learn can significantly affect your progress. Many adults report feeling more in control of their bodies as they navigate through the water.

There might be some initial discomfort associated with swimming, especially if you are not accustomed to physical activity. However, adult swimming classes often emphasize gradual acclimatization to the water, which helps ease anxiety. You can expect to cover essential techniques at a comfortable pace, ensuring each lesson contributes to your overall confidence and ability in the water.

Moreover, adults may bring a unique determination to learn, driven by personal goals or the desire to join family and friends in swimming activities. Whether it is for leisure, travel, or fitness, the motivation to learn enhances the learning experience.

### Health Benefits of Swimming

Engaging in swimming provides a plethora of health benefits almost unmatched by other forms of exercise. For adults, it is crucial to maintain functional fitness as we age, and swimming offers a full-body workout that strengthens the major muscle groups without putting strain on the joints.

- **Cardiovascular Health**: Swimming is an excellent aerobic exercise that increases heart rate while improving circulation and lung capacity.
- **Weight Management**: Regular swimming can support weight loss or maintenance by burning calories and improving metabolism. According to a study by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, "just 30 minutes of swimming can burn approximately 200-300 calories, depending on the intensity."
- **Mental Health**: Beyond the physical benefits, swimming can also enhance mental well-being. The rhythmic nature of swimming can promote relaxation and reduce stress, which is vital in today's fast-paced world. Many swimmers report feeling happier and more rejuvenated after a session in the water.

### Making Swimming a Social Activity

Another advantage of participating in swimming lessons is the opportunity to meet others with similar interests. Adult swimming classes often foster a sense of community, creating friendships that extend beyond the pool.
