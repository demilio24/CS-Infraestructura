Understanding the Real Problem

Why Bath Time Feels So Hard

Your child isn't being difficult. They're doing exactly what their brain is wired to do when something feels unsafe.

💧

Water on the face feels sudden

The sensation is unexpected and overwhelming — their brain reads it as a threat, not just a bath.

🌀

They feel out of control

Kids need to feel in control to feel safe. The moment they lose it, panic takes over — even over something small.

🧠

Past experiences build fear

One scary bath moment can wire the brain for resistance. That's not weakness — that's memory doing its job.

The Method

The Miss Kris Method™
— Step by Step

Follow this sequence every bath, 3–5 pours per session. No skipping. No negotiating. Just calm, confident repetition.

1

Prepare Them

Say "Ready… set… go" before every pour. This gives them a moment to brace and builds trust — they know what's coming.

2

Pour With Confidence

Full pour over the face — no hesitation. If you hesitate, they sense uncertainty and the anxiety grows. Own the moment.

3

No Immediate Towel

Let them blink, breathe, and regulate for a moment. The towel reflex teaches panic — this teaches them they're okay.

4

Praise Immediately

"YOU DID IT! That was amazing!" — enthusiastic, specific, immediate praise locks in the win. Their nervous system needs to hear it.

5

Stay Consistent

Repeat 3–5 times every bath. The win isn't perfection — the win is finishing. That's how confidence is built, one pour at a time.
