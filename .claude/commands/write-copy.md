## THE VOICE — EXTRACTED FROM REAL FUNNELS WE'RE PROUD OF

These patterns come from Charles (Mobile Notary Miami), Wendy (LowCountry Swim School), Becca (AquaSquad Swim), and Ignacio (CS Infraestructura). Every funnel we build should sound like these.

### WHAT THE VOICE SOUNDS LIKE
- Reads like a knowledgeable friend guiding you through a decision, not a brand selling at you
- Warm, direct, and specific. Never vague. Never fluffy.
- Confident without being aggressive. Reassuring without being soft.
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs. White space is a feature.

## ABSOLUTE COPY RULES — NEVER VIOLATE

- Never use em-dashes (—) anywhere
- Never use hyphens as mid-sentence dashes — use "and", "but", or a period
- Never name competitors by name
- Never use fear, doom, or danger language to create urgency
- Never tell readers they wasted money or made bad past choices
- Body copy: max 2 paragraphs per section, 1-2 sentences each
- Never repeat the same idea twice in one section
- Every CTA must have a subtext reassurance line below it
- CTAs are warm and action-specific — never "Submit", "Click Here", or "Sign Up Now"
- Always use exact numbers from the intake form — never be vague when data exists
- Geographic specificity always — name the city, county, or region
- Credentials stated plainly — never bragged, never buried

## SECTION COPY GUIDELINES (same format as Prompt1.txt)

Follow the same 13-section structure and OUTPUT FORMAT from Prompt1.txt, but write every word in the voice above.

Key section-specific voice notes:

**HERO:** Call out the exact audience and location in the eyebrow badge. Headline = the outcome they want, not what you do.

**WHY THIS WORKS DIFFERENTLY:** Acknowledge what they've tried without attacking it. Frame your approach as the natural next step. Tone: "Here's what we've found works better" not "Here's why everything else fails."

**ABOUT:** Write it in first person as if the founder is speaking directly. Origin story first, then approach. End with something human — not a credential list.

**FAQ:** Calm, honest, 1-3 sentences per answer. The tone of someone who's answered this question a hundred times and is happy to answer it again.

**FINAL CTA:** Warm encouragement. "Stop rearranging your day around a notary office. One quick form and we are on our way to you." Not hype. Not pressure. Just clarity and invitation.
