The Problem With Most Swim Schools

Your Child Deserves Better Than Lessons That Take Years

Most parents have already tried swim lessons that promise results but lead to frustration.

Endless Lessons, Slow Progress

Big swim schools want to keep you enrolled forever. Level after level, year after year, without your child ever mastering real skills.

Too Much Support

Instructors hold children constantly. Kids never learn to move independently in the water. That is the one skill that actually saves lives.

"Fun" Over Safety

Programs prioritizing entertainment mean survival skills get ignored. Games are not the same as life-saving technique.

Inexperienced Instructors

Most swim schools use passive instructors evaluated on whether kids are not crying, not on whether they're actually learning to swim.

We Do Things Differently

At Intuitive Swimming, we focus on life-saving skills first. We teach kids to swim intuitively. This is the foundation of a real stroke, and it works because they're more relaxed. Our instructors can get more repetitions in 10 minutes than most can in half an hour. With over 3,000 students and 11 years of experience, we deliver real results that keep your child safe around water.

How It Works

Your Child's Path to Water Safety

From your first call to your child swimming independently, here's what to expect.

1

Schedule Your Intro Assessment

Fill out a quick form and we'll schedule a brief call to learn about your child, answer your questions, and determine if we're the right fit.

2

Complete Your Child's Assessment

We review your child's age, development, and any previous swim experience. This lets us create a personalized instruction plan focused on their specific needs.

3

Begin Lessons

Your child starts their twice-weekly, 15-minute sessions. We focus on core water safety skills first. You'll see progress from week one, with updates to track development.

4

Graduate to Independence

Your child can roll over, float, and find air independently. From here, they can continue with monthly lessons, join Swim Team Prep, or simply enjoy the water with confidence.

Common Questions

Your Questions, Answered

Safety is our top priority. Each child completes a medical and developmental intake before beginning lessons. Instruction is closely monitored, individualized, and adjusted daily based on your child's physical and emotional readiness. We follow strict safety protocols designed to support learning while protecting your child's well-being.
Young children learn motor skills best through short, frequent practice rather than occasional longer lessons. The 15-minute structure allows skills to be introduced, practiced, and reinforced without fatigue or overstimulation. Short lessons also support attention span, body temperature regulation, and overall learning efficiency. This is why we recommend two to three lessons weekly.
Lessons are designed for children 6 months and older, once they are developmentally ready to participate. Starting early allows children to learn water safety skills during the most effective years for motor learning. Readiness is evaluated individually and discussed with families before enrollment.
Every child progresses at a different pace. Many children demonstrate reliable water safety skills within 4 to 6 weeks, while others may require 6 to 8 weeks depending on age, development, and individual readiness. We never rush progression Instruction is always guided by your child's needs.
Parents do not enter the water during lessons. This allows children to develop independent responses without relying on parental cues. As your child progresses, you may be invited into the pool near the end of a session to learn how to safely practice skills at home. Parents remain present, supportive, and engaged throughout every lesson.
