How It Works

Your Child's Path to Water Safety

From your first call to your child swimming independently, here is what to expect.

1

Schedule Your Consultation

Fill out our quick form and we will schedule a brief call to learn about your child, answer your questions, and determine if we are the right fit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Your Questions, Answered

Everything you need to know before getting started.

Safety is our top priority. Each child completes a medical and developmental intake before beginning lessons. Instruction is closely monitored, individualized, and adjusted daily based on your child's physical and emotional readiness. We follow strict safety protocols designed to support learning while protecting your child's well-being.
Young children learn motor skills best through short, frequent practice rather than occasional longer lessons. The 15-minute structure allows skills to be introduced, practiced, and reinforced without fatigue or overstimulation. Short lessons also support attention span, body temperature regulation, and overall learning efficiency. This is why we recommend two to three lessons weekly.
Lessons are designed for children 6 months and older, once they are developmentally ready to participate. Starting early allows children to learn water safety skills during the most effective years for motor learning. Readiness is evaluated individually and discussed with families before enrollment.
Every child progresses at a different pace. Many children demonstrate reliable water safety skills within 4 to 6 weeks, while others may require 6 to 8 weeks depending on age, development, and individual readiness. We never rush progression; instruction is guided by your child's needs.
Parents do not enter the water during lessons. This allows children to develop independent responses without relying on parental cues. As your child progresses, you may be invited into the pool near the end of a session to learn how to safely practice skills at home. Parents remain present, supportive, and engaged throughout every lesson.
No. We encourage parents to stay and observe. Calm, consistent parental presence supports learning and helps your child feel secure in a new environment.
Some children may fuss or cry during early lessons as they adjust to a new environment and new skills. This response typically decreases as children become more confident and familiar with the process. Instruction is always guided by your child's readiness and well-being.
No. There is no such thing as "drown-proof." Our lessons provide an important layer of protection, but active supervision and water safety measures are always required. Survival skills are one part of a comprehensive approach to drowning prevention.
