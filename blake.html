FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Your Questions Answered

Everything you need to know about our water safety programs and what to expect.

We work with children as young as 6 months old. The earlier you start, the better! Young children are naturally comfortable in water and learn survival skills quickly. However, it's never too late to start—we teach children of all ages and skill levels.

Our infant and toddler programs focus on essential survival skills like rolling to float, while older children progress to stroke development and competitive swimming preparation.

Most children complete our survival swim program in 4-7 weeks with consistent daily lessons. This intensive approach delivers results dramatically faster than traditional programs that can take months or years.

The exact timeline depends on your child's age, comfort level, and how consistently they attend lessons. We provide regular progress updates so you always know how your child is advancing.

All of our lessons are private, one-on-one instruction. This personalized approach allows us to focus entirely on your child's specific needs and progress at their own pace.

Private lessons are more effective for learning survival skills because there are no distractions, and the instructor can provide immediate feedback and adjustments tailored to your child.

Each lesson is 10 minutes long, which is the ideal duration for young children to maintain focus and energy. We meet 5 days per week, Monday through Friday.

This daily consistency is key to rapid skill development. Short, frequent lessons are far more effective than longer, less frequent sessions for building muscle memory and confidence.

Your child should wear a regular swimsuit—no flotation devices, swim diapers with layers, or heavy clothing during lessons. We want them to feel the water naturally and develop proper body positioning.

Later in the program, we practice in regular clothes to prepare children for real-world water emergencies. We'll let you know when to bring clothes for this important training.

Some children may cry initially as they adjust to a new experience, but crying doesn't mean they're in distress—it's often just their way of expressing uncertainty about something unfamiliar.

Our experienced instructors know how to comfort children while keeping them focused on learning. Most children adapt quickly, and tears typically stop within the first week as they gain confidence and realize they're safe.

We serve families across Metro Denver including Englewood, Littleton, Aurora, and surrounding areas. Lessons are held in a safe, controlled pool environment designed specifically for swim instruction.

During your assessment call, we'll discuss location options that work best for your schedule and provide exact address details.

Upon completing our survival swim program, your child receives a graduation certificate and is ready to progress to advanced programs or join a competitive swim team.

We provide guidance on maintenance practice, recommendations for local swim teams, and options for continuing stroke development if desired. Many families return for periodic refresher sessions to maintain skills.
